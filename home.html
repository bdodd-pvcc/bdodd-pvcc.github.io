Our Mission

The goal of this website is to provide information about the legal concept of tribal sovereignty, its history, and its current status in the United States.

Tribal sovereignty refers to the right of Native American tribes to govern themselves and make their own laws without interference from outside governments. This right is based on the idea that Native American tribes are sovereign nations with a distinct political and cultural identity; this is recognized by the U.S. Constitution and various legal decisions.

On this website, you'll find information about the history of tribal sovereignty, including its origins and evolution over time. You will also find information on the legal framework that exists to protect and respect tribal sovereignty. These measures include treaties, federal laws, and court decisions.
